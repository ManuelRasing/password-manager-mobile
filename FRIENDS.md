# Password Manager — Setup Guide

Welcome! This is a private password manager. It keeps your passwords encrypted
so only you can read them — not even the server (or Manuel) can see them.

**You'll need:**
- An Android phone
- Your **username** and **API key** (Manuel will send these to you privately)
- The **APK file** (the app installer — Manuel will send a link)

---

## 1. Install the app

1. Tap the APK link Manuel sent you to download it.
2. Open the downloaded file. Android will warn about "installing unknown apps" —
   tap **Settings** → enable **Allow from this source** → go back → **Install**.
3. Open the app once it's installed.

> This warning is normal. The app isn't on the Play Store (it's private), so
> Android asks for permission the first time.

---

## 2. Connect to the server

On first launch you'll land on the **Settings** screen:

1. **Server URL** — already filled in. Leave it as is.
2. **Username** — type the username Manuel gave you (all lowercase).
3. **API Key** — paste the long key Manuel gave you.
4. Tap **Test Connection**. It should say **"Connected & API key verified ✓"**.
   - If it says the server is starting up, wait ~30 seconds and tap again — the
     server "sleeps" when not used and takes a moment to wake.
5. Tap **Save**.

---

## 3. Create your master password

Next you'll set a **master password**. This is the one password you must
remember — it unlocks everything else.

- Choose something strong that you don't use anywhere else.
- **Write it down somewhere safe (on paper).**
- ⚠️ **There is no "forgot password".** If you lose it, your saved passwords
  cannot be recovered — you'd have to start over with a fresh account.

Type it twice and tap **Set Password**.

---

## 4. Turn on fingerprint/face unlock (recommended)

So you don't have to type your master password every time:

1. Go to **Settings** (gear icon, top right).
2. Under **Security**, turn on **Biometric Unlock**.
3. Confirm with your fingerprint/face.

---

## 5. Turn on Autofill (recommended)

This lets the app fill in passwords for you in your browser and other apps:

1. **Biometric Unlock must be on first** (step 4).
2. In **Settings → Security**, tap **Set up Autofill**.
3. Android's settings open — pick **Password Manager** from the list.

Now, when you tap a login field, you'll see a "Unlock Password Manager"
suggestion. Tap it, confirm with your fingerprint, and pick the login.

> **Tip:** After you add or change a password in the app, open the app once so
> autofill picks up the change.

---

## 6. Add your first password

1. On the main screen, tap the **+** button.
2. Fill in the site name, your username/email, and the password (or tap the
   wand icon to generate a strong one).
3. Tap **Save**.

---

## Everyday use

- **See a password:** tap a credential → it shows after unlocking. Tap the copy
  icon to copy it (it auto-clears from the clipboard after 30 seconds).
- **Check your password health:** Settings → **Password Health** shows weak,
  reused, or breached passwords.
- **Works offline:** if you have no signal, the app shows your last-loaded
  passwords. Pull down to refresh when you're back online.

---

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | What to do |
|---|---|
| "Waking up the server…" | Wait ~30 seconds. The free server sleeps when idle. |
| "API key is incorrect" | Double-check the key matches exactly what Manuel sent. |
| Forgot master password | No recovery — ask Manuel to reset your account, then set it up fresh. |
| Autofill not showing | Make sure Biometric Unlock is on, then redo step 5. Open the app once after adding new logins. |
| Screenshot is black | That's intentional — the app blocks screenshots to protect your passwords. |
